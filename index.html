AI for Physics: a Practical Approach

CFD-101

Purpose

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) is a tool for modeling, studying and understanding the effect of Physics on well-defined fluid systems. It is used widely across the industry for a variety of applications, including helping product design that achieve efficient flow penetration, or avoid unpredictable hazardous situations and help mitigate the risk.

Elements of Solvation!

  1. Physics equation

    Describes and governs the phenomenon we want to isolate. Usually intractable as-is, we make hypothesis on the fluid properties to limit the complexity yet keeping relevance of results.

  2. Discretization

    Laws of Physics apply to continuous domain. Over a usually unstructured mesh, constructed by exploiting remarkable symmetries of the system, making strong hypothesis, making non-linear transformations, or projecting the data in the spectral space.

  3. A Method

    For solving the equation

    • Finite Difference Method (FDM)
    • Finite Volume Method (FVM)
    • Finite Element Method (FEM)
    • Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM)
